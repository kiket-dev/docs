You want to model a real business process. You don't want to learn YAML, git, or what a webhook is. Kiket is designed for you to do the first without needing the rest.

## Your starter path

<Steps>

<Step>

### Create a project from a template

Start in the app, click **New project → From template**, and pick the one closest to your process. The template ships a working workflow, a board, and basic issue types — you edit from there instead of starting blank.

Most useful templates for non-technical users: **Kanban**, **Scrum**, **Marketing**, **Sales**, **Product**, **Support**, **Legal**.

</Step>

<Step>

### Customize on the canvas

Every template opens in the visual editor. You can:

- Drag states around, add new ones, delete ones you don't need.
- Click a transition to rename it.
- Click a state to open its inspector — SLAs, approvals, automations, required fields — all in plain-English form fields.

</Step>

<Step>

### Wire in notifications

Settings → Integrations → Marketplace. One-click install Slack or Teams. Now on any state, the **Automations** inspector tab lets you say "on enter, post to #contracts" — no code.

</Step>

<Step>

### Share a dashboard with stakeholders

Dashboards page → **New dashboard**. Drag in widgets (stat, line chart, SLA stoplight). If you need to share with an exec without giving them a seat, toggle **Public sharing** and set a password.

</Step>

</Steps>

## You never need to

- Touch YAML. The canvas writes it; you don't read it.
- Learn git. The app commits on save; you never clone, pull, or push.
- Install anything. Everything happens in the browser.

## You'll occasionally want to

- **Simulate** — the top-right button on every workflow editor drops a dummy issue and walks it through your states. Lets you catch "wait, this transition isn't gated" bugs before real issues hit them.
- **Invite an engineer** — when you hit a ceiling (unusual predicate, custom webhook), pair with someone on the engineering side for 10 minutes. They can do it in YAML while you watch; the change shows up on your canvas immediately.
