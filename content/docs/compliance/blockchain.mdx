---
title: Blockchain Anchoring
description: Cryptographically anchor your audit trail to Polygon for tamper-evident evidence.
---

Every audit entry Kiket records — workflow transitions, approvals, field
edits, comments — is hashed and batched into a Merkle tree. That tree's
root is published to Polygon roughly once an hour. The result: auditors
can prove what happened, **and** prove nothing was changed after the
fact, without trusting Kiket or your database.

## Why it matters

Regulated frameworks (GDPR Art. 30, SOC 2 CC7, HIPAA §164.312(b), ISO
27001 A.12.4) require audit trails that are both complete and resistant
to tampering. Traditional logs satisfy "complete" but not "tamper
evident" — an admin with DB access can rewrite history unnoticed.
Anchoring each batch's Merkle root to a public blockchain makes
post-anchor edits to those entries **mathematically detectable** by any
party holding the entries and the chain.

Two honest caveats:

- **Entries pending their next hourly anchor remain regular DB rows.**
  Until a batch is anchored, tampering with it is not detectable by the
  chain alone. Keep anchor cadence short for the events you care most
  about.
- **Blockchain anchoring proves what was written wasn't changed; it
  does not prove what was *never* written.** Completeness still comes
  from the append-only discipline of the audit logger, not from the
  chain.

## How it works

1. **Hash each entry.** Every row in `audit_logs` is deterministically
   hashed (SHA-256 of the canonical JSON representation).
2. **Batch into a Merkle tree.** A batch (default 100 entries, but any
   size) is folded into a binary Merkle tree.
3. **Publish the root.** The root hash is submitted as a transaction on
   Polygon. Only the 32-byte root hits the chain — your data never
   leaves your database.
4. **Verify anytime.** Given the stored entries and the on-chain root,
   any party can re-compute the tree and confirm it matches.

## UI surfaces

| Location | What you see |
| --- | --- |
| **Compliance → Overview** | One-glance "anchored" badge with relative time. Click to open the chain view. |
| **Compliance → Blockchain tab** | Full table of anchors with per-row verify buttons. |
| **Compliance → Chain** (`/compliance/chain`) | Timeline view with expandable entries, Polygonscan links, and re-verify controls. |

The `AnchorBadge` primitive — shown inline on each anchor — communicates
state in four colors:

- **Anchored** (green, anchor icon) — batch is on-chain.
- **Verified** (green, checkmark) — you re-ran the Merkle proof and it matched.
- **Pending** (gray, clock) — the batch is local-only; next scheduled anchor will include it.
- **Failed** (red, warning) — verification found a tree/root mismatch. **Investigate immediately.**

## Triggering an anchor

Anchors run automatically every hour via a BullMQ scheduler. To force
one now:

1. Open **Compliance → Blockchain** or **Compliance → Chain**.
2. Click **Anchor Now**.
3. Wait a few seconds — the new row appears at the top with its tx
   hash. Clicking the tx hash opens the transaction on Polygonscan.

You can also trigger from the API:

```bash
curl -X POST https://api.kiket.dev/v1/blockchain/anchor \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer $TOKEN" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{"organizationId":"YOUR_ORG_ID"}'
```

## Verification

To prove the chain is intact at any point in the future:

1. Open **Compliance → Chain** and click **Verify** on any row.
2. Kiket re-computes the Merkle tree from the stored entries, compares
   the root against the on-chain transaction, and shows
   `N/N leaves verified`.
3. Export the evidence via **Compliance → Chain → Export** (PDF,
   Excel, or CSV) — auditors typically want the full list with tx
   hashes and entry counts.

## Cost

Polygon transactions cost fractions of a cent. A hundred anchors a
month is under $1 at typical gas prices. If you want to disable
on-chain publishing and keep only the local Merkle commitments, set
`BLOCKCHAIN_ANCHORING_ENABLED=false` in your API env.
